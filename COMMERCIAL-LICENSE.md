## What Counts as Commercial Use?

Commercial use includes, but is not limited to:

- Offering mqvi (or a modified version) as a hosted service (SaaS) for paying customers
- Selling mqvi or a derivative work as a product
- Bundling mqvi into a commercial product or service
- Using mqvi internally at a for-profit organization as a core part of a revenue-generating service

**Non-commercial use** (personal projects, education, non-profit organizations, self-hosting for friends/family) does **not** require a commercial license.

## Commercial License Terms

Commercial licenses are negotiated on a case-by-case basis and typically include:

- **Revenue sharing** based on gross revenue derived from the software
- **Quarterly revenue reporting** obligations
- **Attribution** requirements
- Permission to use the software in closed-source deployments

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I fork and modify mqvi for free?
Yes, as long as you comply with AGPL-3.0 (keep your code open source and under AGPL-3.0). No commercial license needed.

### Can I self-host mqvi for my friends?
Yes, personal and non-commercial self-hosting is free under AGPL-3.0.

### What if I'm not sure whether my use case is commercial?
